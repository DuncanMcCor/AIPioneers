1. Early Life and Education

Born in Boston in 1927, John McCarthy was a mathematical prodigy from a young age. He earned his bachelor's degree in mathematics from the California Institute of Technology (Caltech) in 1948 and went on to receive his Ph.D. in mathematics from Princeton University in 1951. His early academic pursuits laid the groundwork for his revolutionary contributions to computer science and artificial intelligence.

2. The Birth of AI

In 1955, John McCarthy penned a proposal for a summer workshop at Dartmouth College. In this document, he introduced the term "Artificial Intelligence" for the first time, defining the field's core goal as "to proceed on the basis of the conjecture that every aspect of learning or any other feature of intelligence can in principle be so precisely described that a machine can be made to simulate it." The 1956 Dartmouth Summer Research Project on Artificial Intelligence is widely considered the founding event of the field.

3. LISP and Computer Science

McCarthy's influence extends far beyond coining a term. He invented the LISP (LISt Processing) programming language in 1958, which became the second-oldest high-level programming language still in use today. LISP was revolutionary for its time, designed to be symbolic rather than numeric, making it ideal for AI research. He also pioneered the concept of garbage collection, an automatic memory management feature now fundamental to languages like Java and Python.

4. Lasting Legacy

McCarthy's concept of time-sharing, which allows multiple users to access a single computer simultaneously, laid the conceptual groundwork for modern cloud computing. His work on formal logic and commonsense reasoning remains a central pillar of AI research. A recipient of the prestigious Turing Award, John McCarthy is remembered not only as the man who named a field, but as a visionary whose foundational ideas continue to shape the digital world.
